Features

Everything a link needs, the moment you save it

Capture, enrich, organize, and revisit — all on a single private device.

Share to save

Save any link straight from the Android share sheet, or paste one in. MaybeLater fetches the title, thumbnail, and description automatically — oEmbed for sites like YouTube and Vimeo, Open Graph for everything else.

On-device AI

Gemini Nano tags and summarizes each page right on your phone. Toggle it on and page content never touches an AI cloud service — with a heuristic fallback when a device can't run Nano.

Organize your way

Group links into collections, multi-tag them, and find anything with full-text search. Filter by unread, reminders, broken, or duplicates — and switch between a roomy list or a packed grid.

Reminders that stick

Set a time to revisit any link and get a local notification. Reminders survive reboots and stay put until they actually fire — reading a bookmark never clears them by accident.

Broken-link checking

Background health checks flag links that go dead — daily, weekly, biweekly, or monthly — so your library never rots. Filter to just the broken ones, or run a full check on demand.

Yours, and private

Local-first and fully offline. Lock the app with a PIN and optional biometrics, and back up to your own Google Drive with end-to-end encryption. There's no MaybeLater server holding your data.

Capture

Share it once, get all the details

Send a link from any app and MaybeLater does the rest — pulling in the page title, a thumbnail, and a description, and flagging duplicates when a link normalizes to one you already saved. Add URLs manually too, and edit the title, tags, collection, notes, and reminder any time.

Intelligence, on-device

Smart tags and summaries that never leave your phone

MaybeLater uses Gemini Nano and ML Kit to auto-tag saved pages (you choose the max number of tags) and to write a short summary you can skim before diving in — with live progress as it works. It's device-gated, so summaries only appear where Nano is supported, and page content stays entirely on the device.

Find

A library that organizes itself

Browse by collection or by tag with live counts, run a full-text search, and slice the list with filters for All, Has-reminder, Unread, Read, Broken, or Duplicate. Sort by recency, pick a list or grid layout, and multi-select to act on bookmarks in bulk.

Peace of mind

Private by default, backed up by choice

Everything lives in an on-device database and works with no connection. Add an app lock — a PIN with optional fingerprint or face unlock and an auto-lock timeout — and turn on scheduled, end-to-end encrypted backups to your own Google Drive. Prefer files? Export and import your bookmarks as plain or password-encrypted JSON.

FAQ

Good questions

Does MaybeLater work offline?

Yes. It's local-first — every bookmark lives in an on-device database, so browsing, editing, and searching all work with no connection. The network is only used to fetch page metadata, run link-health checks, and (if you enable it) back up.

Do I need an account?

No. There's no MaybeLater account and no MaybeLater server. Cloud backup is entirely optional and, when enabled, saves encrypted files to your own Google Drive.

Is the AI private?

Yes. Auto-tagging and summaries run on-device via Gemini Nano and ML Kit — the content of the pages you save is never sent to an AI cloud service. On devices that don't support Nano, tagging falls back to a local heuristic and summaries simply aren't shown.

Which Android versions are supported?

Android 10 (API level 29) and newer, on phones and tablets.

What does MaybeLater cost?

MaybeLater is free. There are no in-app purchases and no subscriptions — every feature, including on-device AI and encrypted backup to your own Google Drive, is included.

What data does MaybeLater collect?

Your bookmarks stay on your device. The app sends only anonymous, content-free usage analytics and crash reports (via Google Firebase) — never your URLs, titles, notes, or content. Full details are in the Privacy Policy.
